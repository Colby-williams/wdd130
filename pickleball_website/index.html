History of Pickleball

Basic Rules of Pickleball

1. Court and Gear: It’s played on a badminton-sized area measuring 20 by 44 feet with a net standing 36 inches high on the sides and 34 inches in the middle. Players use paddles and a ball that has holes.
2. Serve: The serve needs an underhand motion, paddle below the waist, and must be aimed diagonally into the opponent's designated service area.
3. Two-Bounce Rule: The ball has to bounce once on each side before players can hit it in mid-air without waiting for it to bounce first.
4. Non-Volley Zone (Kitchen): Players can’t strike the ball while positioned in the non-volley zone, which is a 7-foot space beside the net.
5. Scoring: Points are scored only by the serving side. Games are usually played to 11 points, requiring a team to win by a margin of 2 points.

Benefits of Playing Pickleball

1. Heart Health: Pickleball offers a quality workout, boosting cardiovascular health and stamina.
2. Strength Gains: The game involves many actions that build muscle and bone strength, lowering osteoporosis risk.
3. Balance and Coordination: Quick moves and coordination during play sharpen balance and help prevent falls.
4. Mental Well-being: The social nature of the game can lower stress and enhance mood, aiding overall mental health.
5. For Everyone: Pickleball is easy to grasp. It’s suitable for all ages and fitness levels, making it a fun way to stay active.
